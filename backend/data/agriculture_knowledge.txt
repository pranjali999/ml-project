Kharif crops are sown at the onset of monsoon rains and harvested in autumn. Common examples include rice, maize, cotton, and millets. Water availability during establishment is critical.

Rabi crops are winter crops that rely on residual soil moisture and irrigation. Wheat, barley, mustard, and chickpea are typical Rabi crops in India.

Zaid crops are short-duration summer crops grown between Rabi and Kharif, such as watermelon, cucumber, and some pulses where irrigation is available.

Soil testing every two to three years helps optimize fertilizer use, reduce cost, and limit environmental losses. Macronutrients NPK and micronutrients should be balanced based on soil analysis.

Drip and sprinkler irrigation improve water use efficiency compared to flood irrigation, especially in water-stressed districts.

Integrated pest management combines host resistance, cultural practices, biological controls, and judicious chemical use based on scouting thresholds.

Climate risk management includes diversifying crops, adjusting sowing dates, using stress-tolerant varieties, and monitoring seasonal forecasts.

Post-harvest losses can be reduced through proper drying, storage hygiene, and timely marketing.
